Pokémon Showdown is an open-source simulator that brings Pokémon's turn-based combat to a competitive community with thousands of daily players. Competitive Pokémon battles are two-player, stochastic, imperfect-information games. Players build teams of Pokémon and compete head-to-head. Turn outcomes are uncertain and governed by complex mechanics with endless special cases. Success depends on more than just handling randomness: players must model opponents and make decisions under uncertainty. Key details about the opponent's team remain hidden until they directly affect the battle, forcing players to infer information and predict moves from past interactions. Expert human players excel by anticipating their opponent's strategies. This combination of randomness, partial observability, and team diversity presents a daunting challenge for AI agents that stresses the ability to plan and generalize.

While Pokémon Showdown "battle bots" have a long history, powerful language models, large-scale reinforcement learning datasets, and open-source resources have driven a fresh surge in interest from the ML research community. Recent methods have achieved human-level performance in popular singles rulesets. How much further can we push the boundaries of Competitive Pokémon AI? Join Track 1 of the PokéAgent Challenge to help find out!

Rules and Submission Guidelines

To avoid disrupting human players, PokéAgent participants will compete on an AI-only Showdown server. Participants will battle against organizer baselines (and each other!) on a ranked ladder to qualify for a tournament held at the end of the competition window.


Formal rules and timelines for the ladder and tournament stages will be announced by June 25th, 2025, and the Showdown server will launch on July 7th, 2025. Until then, participants are free to experiment on a self-hosted server against baselines provided by the starter kit!
