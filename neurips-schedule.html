NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

December 7th, 2025

8:00 AM – 10:45 AM PST

NeurIPS Virtual Event Page

Workshop Schedule

8:00 – 8:30 AM

The PokeAgent Challenge: Competitive and Long Context Learning at Scale

Seth Karten (Princeton University)
Opening presentation introducing the PokeAgent Challenge, its scientific relevance, and key research frontiers in game AI.

8:30 – 9:00 AM

Invited Talk 1: Game Theory and Imperfect Information

Aaron Traylor (Unaffiliated, currently at Microsoft)
A deep dive into strategic decision-making under imperfect information, drawing from game theory principles and their applications in AI systems.

9:00 – 9:30 AM

Winner Awards + Lightning Presentations

Top Teams (5 minutes each)
Celebration and recognition of winning teams with brief presentations showcasing their innovative approaches and solutions.

9:30 – 10:00 AM

Invited Talk 2: Gemini Game Effort and Kaggle Game Arena

Minmin Chen (Google DeepMind)
Exploring Google DeepMind's game AI initiatives, including the Gemini project and Kaggle's Game Arena platform.

10:00 – 10:30 AM

Panel Discussion: Research Frontiers in Game AI

Moderators: Jake Grigsby (UT Austin) & Seth Karten (Princeton)
Panelists: Stephanie Milani (NYU / Johns Hopkins), Fei Fang (CMU), Chi Jin (Princeton), Minmin Chen (Google DeepMind), Aaron Traylor (Unaffiliated, currently at Microsoft)
An engaging discussion on the future of game AI research, emerging challenges, and opportunities for advancing decision-making systems.

10:30 – 10:45 AM

Closing Remarks

Chi Jin (Princeton University)
Final thoughts and reflections on the competition, with a look ahead to future directions in AI research.
